FAQ

What are the primary capabilities of Curiosity and Perseverance?

Both rovers carry cameras, spectrometers, and instruments to analyze rock and soil chemistry, mineralogy, and the environment. Perseverance also carries mechanisms for sample caching and houses instruments designed to detect organic compounds and biosignatures.

What are the main mission goals?

Curiosity's primary goal was to determine whether Gale Crater ever offered conditions favorable for microbial life. Perseverance's goals include searching for evidence of past life, collecting samples for return, and testing technologies for future exploration.

How do these missions tie into a future manned mission?

Rovers provide critical data on surface conditions, geology, hazards, and resource locations. That data helps engineers design life support systems, landing strategies, and mission profiles for future human missions to Mars.
